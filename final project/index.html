Stroke Predictor analysis

Stroke is a serious medical condition that occurs when blood flow to the brain is disrupted, leading to brain cell damage. It can have devastating consequences, affecting both individuals and society as a whole.

Every year, more than 795,000 people in the United States experience a stroke. Approximately 610,000 of these cases are first or new strokes, while around 185,000 are recurrent strokes (in people who have previously had a stroke). Stroke is a leading cause of serious long-term disability. It significantly reduces mobility in more than half of stroke survivors aged 65 and older. Sadly, someone in the US dies from a stroke approximately every 3 minutes and 14 seconds. Stroke-related costs in the US totaled nearly $56.5 billion between 2018 and 2019. These costs include healthcare services, stroke medications, and lost workdays.

Therefore, Stroke prevention, awareness, and rapid response are critical. By understanding the impact of stroke, we can take proactive steps to reduce its occurrence and improve outcomes for affected individuals.
